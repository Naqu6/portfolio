I'm Ryan. I’m the oldest of 4 siblings. Growing up, I lived in Toronto, Vancouver, and Palo Alto. I started programming in third grade when my Dad wanted to learn Python through Code Academy. He burnt out once the topic of arrays came up, but I quickly branched out, teaching myself Java and learning about networking from Programming for Dummies books.

I continued programming, building side projects and working professionally. I started interning at 13, and have been working since. At 5 different companies, I've worked in Golang, Java, Objective-C, Swift, PHP, Python, and Javascript (JQuery) in environments built around databases such as MongoDB and MySQL.

I lead my first two professional engineering projects when I was 15 during my internship at League . The first of which was an internal tool that automated the writing of Web Unit Tests and the second a larger-scale computer vision project aimed to help increase automation. Since that internship, I've done Freelance Web Development and used my engineering knowledge to help various local community service organizations. Currently, I'm employed part time as a Full Stack Software Engineer at IDEO.

Personally, I spent a lot of my own time working on side projects. I’ve taught myself more languages than I can count. Often, my evenings are spend fiddling with projects or learning a new programming language. In addition to coding, I’m an avid rock climber, skier, and an FAA licensed Private Pilot. I've been climbing since I was 13 and flying since I was 14. I flew a plane alone before I drove a car alone. If I'm not programming, I'm probably doing something adventurous at a high altitude.

I also enjoy playing with my dog and going on hikes with family.
